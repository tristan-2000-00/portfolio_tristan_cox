The winter sports industry is a thriving and growing community, with millions of people whose work and enjoyment are dependent on accurate weather data. With a large number of apps that offer weather data such as Apple Weather, The National Weather Service, and OnTheSnow Ski & Riding Reports, the weather data differences between every source can be misleading and dividing.

FREEZE provide users with a comprehensive and informative ski weather application that compiles weather data from multiple sources into one user friendly app. This includes real time weather updates, snow conditions, and interactive maps.

Native to both iOs and Android.

Overview

Who is the App for?

This app is designed for people who work or play in the ski and snowboarding industry who are looking for an application that offers accurate and informative weather data.

What kind of tasks can the user perform with the app?

Users will be able to create an account, filter through regions of the US, search for resorts in each state or province, receive accurate weather data, and interact with weather maps. They will also be able to save resorts for quick access for convenience.

Where will your audience use your app?

Users will use my app while at home, on the mountain, or anywhere the user wants to look at weather information for different ski resorts.

User Flows

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

Each set of screens were designed to follow either iOS Human Interface Guidelines or M3 Material guidelines. Native design patterns were used to allow for a seamless fit for each platform.

Rapid Prototype Testing

I created a prototype for the iOS and Android version of my app and had users test it out.

Users were given tasks to complete which included creating an account, manually searching for a resort, and using the main filtering system to find the mountain, Sugarloaf.

Typography & Colors

Typography and color played a pivotal role in shaping my app's visual identity. Not only did it help define my app, it was crucial in following iOS and Android design guidelines.
